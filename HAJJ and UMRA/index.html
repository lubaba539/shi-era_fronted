HAJJ AND UMRAH TRAVELATION

  • HAJJ is required for all muslims with financial and physical means to do.
  • UMRAH, is considered a recommendation, not an obligation, by some branches of islam.
  • Muslims are called to undertake two journeys of faith at least once in their lives.

Hajj

  • HAJJ takes place during the same period each year-during the month os DHUL HIJJAH.
Date Ritual
8th Dhu al-Hijjah Ihram - Entering the state of purity.
8th Dhu al-Hijjah Tawaf - Circling the Kaaba.
9th Dhu al-Hijjah Standing at Arafat - Day of prayer.
10th Dhu al-Hijjah Muzdalifah - Collecting pebbles.
10th Dhu al-Hijjah Ramy al-Jamarat - Throwing stones.
10th Dhu al-Hijjah Eid al-Adha - Festival of sacrifice.
11th-13th Dhu al-Hijjah Ramy al-Jamarat - Continuing the stone throwing.

Umrah

UMRAH, the lesser pilgrimage, can be performed at any time of the year, except the days of HAJJ.

  • Umrah is a voluntary pilgrimage that can be performed at any time of the year. Below are the key rituals:
    • Ihram: Entering the state of purity and wearing special garments.
    • Tawaf: Circling the Kaaba seven times.
    • Sa'i: Walking seven times between the hills of Safa and Marwah.
